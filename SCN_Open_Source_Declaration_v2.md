## Part I — What This Protocol Is

The Sovereign Collectible Network (SCN) is an open-source, ownerless blockchain protocol for authenticating, trading, and verifying physical and digital collectibles. It is not a company. It is not a product. It is not an investment vehicle. It is a protocol — a set of rules that anyone may choose to follow, in the same way anyone may choose to follow the rules of chess or the rules of the HTTP protocol.

SCN came into existence through the voluntary act of its creators publishing its source code and concepts into the public domain on April 5, 2026. From that moment forward, the creators relinquished all ownership, control, and authority over the protocol. The protocol now belongs to the commons — to every person on Earth equally, including the people who created it.

The creators retain no special rights, no governance power, no treasury access, and no ability to modify the protocol unilaterally. They are participants, like everyone else. They hold tokens, like everyone else. Their tokens carry no more weight than any other token holder's tokens.

## Part II — What This Protocol Is Not

This section exists to prevent mischaracterization by any party, including regulatory bodies, corporate competitors, and courts.

**SCN is not a securities offering.** No one is selling SCN tokens as an investment. The protocol exists independently of any sale. Tokens are a utility within the protocol — they represent participation rights, not ownership stakes in any enterprise. There is no enterprise. There is no profit-sharing. There is no management team whose efforts token holders are relying upon. The *Howey* test does not apply because there is no "common enterprise" and no "efforts of others" — the protocol runs on code, not on human management.

**SCN is not a company or foundation.** There is no SCN Inc., no SCN Foundation, no SCN LLC. No legal entity controls this protocol. This is intentional and permanent. The protocol is governed by its code and by the voluntary consensus of its participants, in the same way Bitcoin is governed.

**SCN is not a franchise acquisition vehicle.** The protocol does not exist to purchase sports franchises, leagues, or licensing rights. The Community Commons Reserve — the treasury accumulated from micro-transaction fees — exists solely to fund the maintenance, security, and open-source development of the protocol itself. What the community chooses to do with those funds, after the governance thresholds described in Part IV are met, is the community's decision alone. No direction is prescribed. No acquisition is planned. No league is targeted.

**SCN is not a threat to any existing business.** The protocol is additive, not competitive. It creates new economic activity — new ways for collectors, artists, and athletes to interact with their own property — without displacing any existing market. Any business, including Fanatics, Topps, or any other collectibles company, is free to build on top of SCN, integrate with it, or ignore it entirely.

## Part IV — Governance: The Verified Commons Model

Because SCN has no owner and no foundation, governance is handled entirely by the protocol's code and by the voluntary participation of verified community members. The following rules are encoded in the protocol and cannot be changed by any individual — they can only be changed by a supermajority vote of verified participants after the genesis lock period.

### 4.1 The Genesis Lock

For the first **90 days** after the protocol's mainnet launch, the Community Commons Reserve is locked. No deployment votes can be proposed or executed during this period. This prevents any early actor — including the protocol's creators — from capturing the treasury before a genuine community has formed.

### 4.2 Verification Tiers

Governance participation is weighted by verification tier. Verification is earned through on-chain activity and voluntary identity attestation — it cannot be purchased.

| Tier | Label | How Earned | Governance Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Collector | Owns at least one verified card on-chain | 1 vote per verified card |
| **Tier 2** | Artist | Verified as a card artwork creator (on-chain attestation) | 3 votes per verified card created |
| **Tier 3** | Athlete / Creator | Verified NIL participant (athlete or content creator) | 5 votes per verified NIL card |

### 4.3 Deployment Threshold Gate

The Community Commons Reserve cannot be deployed for any purpose until **both** of the following thresholds are met simultaneously:

**Threshold A — Time:** The 90-day genesis lock has expired.

**Threshold B — Participation:** At least 1,000 Tier 1 accounts, 50 Tier 2 accounts, and 10 Tier 3 accounts have completed on-chain verification. This ensures the community is real and diverse before any funds move.

Once both thresholds are met, the community may vote on any use of the reserve — protocol development, security audits, community grants, or anything else the community chooses. No use is prescribed. No use is prohibited except uses that would violate the ownerless nature of the protocol (e.g., the community cannot vote to assign ownership of the protocol to any individual or entity).

### 4.4 Anti-Inflation Rules

The SCN token has a **fixed maximum supply** established at genesis. No new tokens can ever be minted after genesis. This rule is enforced by the protocol's code and cannot be overridden by any vote, including a supermajority vote. It is a constitutional rule, not a governance rule.

The Community Commons Reserve accumulates tokens from the protocol's 2% transaction fee. These tokens come from existing circulation — they are not newly minted. The total supply never increases.

## Part V — The Community Liquidity Reserve

The term "liquidity pool" carries specific legal and financial connotations that do not accurately describe SCN's mechanism. The correct term is **Community Liquidity Reserve** (CLR).

The CLR is a portion of the Community Commons Reserve that the community may vote to allocate for the purpose of ensuring that SCN tokens can always be exchanged for other assets at a fair market rate. It is not a yield-generating instrument. It is not an investment. It is a public utility — analogous to a central bank's foreign exchange reserve, but governed by community vote rather than by a government.

Participation in the CLR is voluntary. No token holder is required to contribute to or interact with the CLR. Its existence does not make SCN tokens a security, because the CLR does not promise returns — it provides a service (exchangeability) that benefits all token holders equally.

## Part VIII — Governing Principles (Immutable)

These principles are the protocol's constitution. They can be read by anyone and enforced by everyone.

**Principle 1 — No Owner.** The protocol has no owner. No individual, company, foundation, or DAO may claim ownership of the protocol, its code, its brand, or its governance.

**Principle 2 — No Prescribed Purpose.** The Community Commons Reserve has no prescribed purpose. The community decides. The protocol does not prescribe what the community should want.

**Principle 3 — Fixed Supply.** The token supply is fixed at genesis. No inflation. No new minting. Ever.

**Principle 4 — Voluntary Participation.** Everything in this protocol is voluntary. No one is required to use it, hold its tokens, participate in governance, or contribute to the reserve. It is a commons, not a mandate.

**Principle 5 — Burn is Consent.** No card can be burned without the physical act of tearing the card's security strip. Physical destruction is always a voluntary, irreversible act by the card's owner.

**Principle 6 — Code is Law.** The protocol's rules are enforced by its code, not by any human authority. No individual — including the protocol's creators — can override the code unilaterally.

**Principle 7 — Open Forever.** The protocol's source code will always be publicly available under the MIT License. This cannot be changed. Closed-source forks are permitted but are not SCN.
